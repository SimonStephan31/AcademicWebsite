Simon Stephan

Research Scientist in the field of Cognitive Science at the

University of Göttingen

I am a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Göttingen, working in Michael Waldmann’s Lab of Cognitive and Decision Sciences. I’m particularly interested in studying human causal learning and reasoning. Given that causal relationships are neither directly observable nor logically deducible, how do reasoners manage to learn about causes and effect so successfully? How do they use this knowledge to make predictions, diagnoses, or to explain?

On this website, I share with you information about my academic life. You will find things like an up-to-date CV, my publication list, accomplishments, and a list informing you about recent/ upcoming presentations (e.g., conference talks). I also have a litte “posts” section in which I share (mostly) scientific news I consider interesting and worth spreading.
