About me

I am a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Göttingen, working in Michael Waldmann’s Lab of Cognition and Decision Sciences. I’m particularly interested in studying human causal learning and reasoning. Given that causal relationships are neither directly observable nor logically deducible, how do reasoners manage to learn about causes and effect so successfully? How do they use this knowledge to make predictions, diagnoses, or to explain?
